# WHISPERS IN THE DARKNESS

## A Campaign for Swords & Darkness

---

This campaign is built around a discovery. The campaign is about what that discovery does to everyone who hears about it.

What has been found: a section of the Vysandri underground railway running west from the Bellarian town of Kalazym into the mountains of Vysandárien. The railway's existence is documented — three sources in the Chroniclers' King's Hold collection describe it, and one they have misplaced is more specific than they know. What makes the find significant is where this particular section leads. The tunnels do not dead-end in a collapsed station. Six kilometres past the last mapped point, they open into the largest intact Vysandri ruins accessible from the living world: two buried cities, connected by rail, sitting above obsidian deposits that would rewrite the Kilo price table for a generation if brought to market.

Every faction with intelligence operations in the region has heard something. None has a team inside yet. That window will close.

---

## KALAZYM

The last Bellarian town of any size before the frontier gives way to mountains no government has formally claimed since the Vysandri disappeared, Kalazym began as a provisioning stop for Dead Lands expeditions staged from Wintergate's western routes. It grew because staging expeditions requires support infrastructure, and support infrastructure attracts people who have decided that equipping someone else to go somewhere dangerous is preferable to going themselves. The economy runs on that calculation: cold-weather provisions, cartographic services, document assessment, and the specific information brokerage that arises wherever licensed and unlicensed activity share geography.

Bellarian crown authority is present in the form of a garrison of fourteen soldiers and a magistrate who has learned to ask fewer questions per year as her tenure has extended. The Chroniclers do not maintain an office here — Kalazym sits just outside the operational radius they have chosen to formally cover — but they maintain an observer who has presented herself as a Wintergate trading house assessor for eleven years. The fiction is not believed by anyone. It gives everyone the legal cover not to react to it.

The Crimson Company has operated out of a series of connected warehouses in the lower quarter for at least two decades. Their actual business runs through the first three kilometres of the railway tunnel west of town — a natural depot that connects to Wightport's distribution network without touching any customs manifest. Until six weeks ago, their people had never pushed past the third station. There was no commercial reason to go further.

There was never a commercial reason to go further. There was always something there.

---

## THE TWINS

The two peaks visible on clear days above the western ridge are called The Twins. The name is local and informal, old enough that no one in Kalazym can say when it started. The ruins beneath those peaks appear in no published record under any name.

**Tol Dovkas** sits over a network of volcanic chambers, its forges drawing on geothermal energy that has not diminished in eleven centuries. The forge complexes are the most sophisticated metalworking infrastructure ever documented — capable of metal-fusion processes that the Order of Magnus has been theorizing about for two generations without achieving. Vysandri automata, bronze and steel constructs running on maintenance protocols that have never been switched off, patrol the upper levels. Lower down, in the sections where the heat becomes significant, something else is present. The three-person team that reached the lowest forge level came back two people. Their account of what they encountered is incomplete, partly because they stopped mid-sentence and partly because the session ended there.

**Dûmna** lies 120 kilometres deeper into the Vysandri range, accessible by railway from Tol Dovkas. Built into natural cavern systems expanded to a scale that makes "architecture" feel inadequate, the city holds a library of petrified books — the Vysandri's preservation method for significant texts, which converted organic material to stone rather than allowing decay. Reading them requires magic. Accessing what is written requires understanding a script that two people in the Known World can partially parse. The deep sections of the archive — sealed, requiring keys the company will not immediately find — contain something the Vysandri themselves considered dangerous enough to lock away. The Abyssal Resonance in Dûmna runs higher than in Tol Dovkas. What that means for spellcasters is addressed in the Ruins chapter.

Both cities were built to a scale the Vysandri required and humans do not. Ceilings in the public areas average eight metres. Mechanisms are calibrated for hands with different reach and grip. Mapping these spaces by torchlight, at standard exploration pace, is not a trivial undertaking.

---

## THE GIANTS

The Vysandri had their minds taken from them. The records do not describe how. The records that might describe it are in Dûmna's sealed archive.

What is documented in multiple independent sources from the early Third Era is the result: the Vysandri are present in the Known World as the giants that wander the northern territories. Enormous, aimless, occasionally dangerous when startled, treated by most governments as a geographical inconvenience rather than a people. They do not know what they are. Whatever was taken from them was the specific thing that allowed them to recognize themselves — and so they walk through ruins they built without recognizing them, touch mechanisms calibrated for their hands without understanding why the touch feels almost right, stand in chambers whose dimensions fit them as nothing else in the world fits them, and continue doing what they always do.

A giant in Tol Dovkas is a giant sitting in the dark beside a forge that does not respond to its hands. Present the fact plainly. The ruins are made for the thing it used to be, and the thing it used to be is gone in the particular way that leaves everything else intact.

What the company does with this — whether they tell anyone, whether they tell no one — has no mechanical weight until the players give it weight. It is the campaign's genuine question beneath all the faction noise, and the GM should let it surface in its own time.

---

## ABYSSAL RESONANCE IN THE TWINS

The Vysandri built both cities above energy sources the Known World has since forgotten how to identify. The residual resonance — compounded by the geographic proximity to where the Darkness was banished — makes the Twins hostile to active spellcasting in ways that scale with depth and disturbance. Full mechanics are in the Ruins chapter.

What the GM should know before the first session inside the tunnels: do not announce the danger. Let the setting establish its own terms. The first time a spellcaster uses magic in a flagged high-resonance area, nothing happens except the sensation — pressure behind the eyes, the feeling of something adjusting its attention nearby. The second time, the saving throw happens. The consequences are real and escalating from there.

---

## THE FACTION STANDING SYSTEM

This campaign uses the faction standing rules from _Swords & Darkness_ Book I, Chapter 17. Standing with each faction tracks on the Unknown / Known / Trusted / Embedded scale and shifts based on contracts completed, contracts refused, and the specific relationship events detailed in each faction entry below.

Standing is tracked by the GM. The players understand their reputation through how NPCs behave, what contracts are offered, and what information gets shared. The scale is never announced. The consequences announce themselves.

---

## THE FOUR MOVEMENTS

The campaign is structured as four acts, named after musical tempo markings. These are pacing tools for the GM — not a plot outline. Each movement has a central question and a starting condition. The endpoint of each movement is determined by what the company has done.

**Allegro — Whispers in the Depths** _(5–10 sessions)_ Central question: Who controls what has been found? The railway's deep section is not yet common knowledge. The company has a window of advantage. What they do with it — who they tell, what they take, how they handle the Crimson Company's existing claim — sets every faction relationship that follows.

**Adagio — Bets on the Table** _(8–15 sessions)_ Central question: Who controls the railway and its cities? The secret is out. Every contract accepted strengthens someone and weakens someone else. There are no neutral choices; the Twins are too valuable for neutrality to survive.

**Scherzo — Resonance** _(5–10 sessions)_ Central question: What did the company's choices make? The balance of power has shifted. Factions that were damaged take direct action. The ruins' disturbances may have drawn attention that has no faction affiliation.

**Finale — [Title Emerges]** _(2–5 sessions)_ Central question: How does this end? The GM prepares the resolution appropriate to the trajectory that emerged. The company does not choose which ending is available to them. They already chose.

# THE FACTIONS

---

## THE CRIMSON COMPANY

_Wightport-based smuggling operation. Has operated in the Kalazym tunnel network for two decades without knowing what the tunnels connect to._

The Company's interest in the Twins is a logistics question. They have been moving material through these tunnels for twenty years, and the discovery that their depot connects to something considerably larger than anyone realized is an expansion opportunity — a railway system linking two intact Vysandri cities to Wightport's distribution network, bypassing Chronicler documentation requirements entirely. Contraband moving through the tunnel system would be the most significant expansion of their operation since the Company established dominance in Winter's Bay three generations ago.

Their representative in Kalazym, **Ulrik Fendt**, has run the warehouse operation for eight years. He is competent, careful about documentation, and genuinely concerned: two of his people pushed past the third station six weeks ago and one of them came back describing what she called "the most controlled fear I have ever managed." He has not reported the full extent of the discovery up the chain. He is waiting to understand it better. The window for that understanding is closing.

**What they offer:** Unmarked coin, no documentation trail. Access to Wightport's black market for equipment the Wintergate customs house would not pass. Protection from smaller threats in the tunnel network. Knowledge of which sections the Company has already mapped.

**What they demand:** Discretion. Survey information delivered to the Company before anyone else. Willingness to work in spaces where Chronicler authority does not apply. Eventually, exclusivity — not in the first contract, but the ask will come.

**Complications:** The Company's tunnel claim predates the discovery and their operational standing in Bellarian frontier territory is, under an arrangement no one has formally challenged, better than anyone else's. The Chroniclers do not recognize this. The Bellarian crown has never formally adjudicated it. Every other faction is currently trying to exploit that jurisdictional gap in different directions.

**Faction Clock:**

1. Ulrik reports the full extent of the discovery to the Council of Sea Wolves
2. A senior captain arrives in Kalazym to assess the operation personally
3. The Company establishes a forward depot at the second Twins station
4. Direct confrontation with the Chroniclers over tunnel access rights
5. Attempted exclusive control of the railway's Bellarian section
6. Monopolization — or forced out by coalition opposition

---

## THE CHRONICLERS

_King's Hold-based. Institutional authority on Dead Lands access. Neutral by policy; ruthless in practice._

The Chroniclers knew the railway existed. Their surveys have focused on the Bal'mekh end — the stations at Zyzak and Dymoras are documented — and the question of what lies between that end and Bellaria was classified as low commercial priority eleven years ago. That classification is about to change.

Their observer in Kalazym, **Borte Khasar**, presents as a Wintergate trading house assessor. Nobody has believed this for eleven years. She has excellent notes on every expedition that has passed through Kalazym in that period and a courier route to King's Hold that runs four days faster than the crown's post. She knows a discovery has been made. She does not yet know its extent. She has been authorized to offer preliminary contracts while she learns more.

**What they offer:** Certified promissory notes redeemable at full value at King's Hold, and at a ten percent discount everywhere else. Legal protection under Chronicler expedition licensing — meaningless in Vysandárien territory, significant everywhere else. Access to their existing survey data on the railway's Bal'mekh end: partial, but more than the company currently has.

**What they demand:** Survey data submitted to King's Hold. Significant finds documented and transferred to Chronicler institutional custody. No sale of discovered material to any party before Chronicler first refusal. At higher standing: information exclusivity.

**Complications:** The Chroniclers' neutrality doctrine requires them to be useful to multiple powers without belonging to any. They cannot openly oppose any faction in Kalazym without compromising the doctrine. What they can do — and do — is apply operational pressure: documentation requirements, customs interventions, the weight of an organization that controls the legal framework for Dead Lands access. They will not fight the Crimson Company openly. They will make it progressively more expensive to be associated with the Crimson Company.

**Faction Clock:**

1. Borte Khasar requests authorization to expand her operation in Kalazym
2. A senior Chronicler expedition team arrives for independent survey
3. Formal licensing demands served to all active parties in the tunnel
4. Chroniclers file a documented claim on the Twins under the Dead Lands access framework
5. Enforcement action against unlicensed parties — confiscations, legal challenges
6. Full operational control of the railway's Bellarian section — or formal dispute with the crown

---

## THE LONGKEEL CONSORTIUM

_Val'arian commercial operation, southern-faction backed. In Kalazym to find an obsidian source that bypasses Chronicler licensing._

The Val'arian civil war runs in part on the question of who benefits from Val'arian navigational expertise. The southern faction at Shadowport has concluded that the answer, currently, is mostly other people. A Val'arian-controlled obsidian supply, extracted from a site outside Chronicler jurisdiction and distributed through Val'arian maritime routes, would change that negotiation. The Longkeel Consortium is the vehicle for that calculation.

They arrived in Kalazym eight days ago under the cover of a navigational survey contract from a Bellarian merchant house that has no knowledge of what the Consortium is actually doing. Their field director, **Sira Longkeel**, is a former deep-sea navigator who knows the Inner Sea better than she knows dry land. She is conducting the tunnel survey with the same methodical attention she would give to charting an unknown approach channel. The ruins' history does not interest her. The obsidian does.

**What they offer:** Payment in obsidian pre-certified for Val'arian maritime export — worth full Kilo value in markets the Consortium reaches, but requiring the seller to account for provenance when selling elsewhere. Training in tunnel navigation methods adapted from maritime surveying, which is more practical than it sounds. Access to Val'arian distribution networks that operate outside Chronicler documentation.

**What they demand:** Survey contracts with full data exclusivity. Obsidian samples for geological assessment. Confidentiality about their identity and backing. Eventually: escort for extraction equipment.

**Complications:** The Consortium's presence in Bellarian frontier territory is not licensed. Their funding comes from Val'arian commercial interests mid-civil war, which means the backing could shift if the political situation at home changes. Sira Longkeel is competent and careful, but she is operating under instructions from people who have never been inside these tunnels and whose risk assessments are therefore wrong in ways she is gradually discovering.

**Faction Clock:**

1. Complete geological survey of accessible obsidian deposits
2. Establish secure communication with Shadowport backers
3. Negotiate extraction rights through one of the other factions — or pursue independently
4. Begin small-scale obsidian removal through the Crimson Company tunnel network
5. Confrontation with the Chroniclers or the Bellarian crown over unlicensed extraction
6. Extraction at scale — or expulsion from the region

## THE COLLEGE OF WHISPERS

_Operating through bards and information assets. Present in Kalazym before the discovery and specifically interested in what Dûmna's archive contains._

The College's structural interest — preventing any single faction from achieving dominance — is real and shapes their choices here. Their immediate interest is more specific: Thel'marak's archive contains Vysandri knowledge that predates the Second Era. What that knowledge holds about the Darkness's banishment, about the relationship between Vysandri infrastructure and the Five's work, about what was actually done and at what cost — the College has been operating on incomplete information about that event for eleven centuries. The Forbidden Archive in Dûmna is the closest accessible primary source that has ever been found.

They have a bard in Kalazym. Their current operative, **Tariq Montara**, performs at the two establishments in town worth performing at and spends his days listening. He has been in contact with the College's Middle Circle since the initial discovery report reached him a month ago. He is authorized to recruit, to offer contracts, and to share a carefully selected subset of what the College knows about Vysandri history in exchange for what the company finds.

**What they offer:** Partial translation support for Vysandri script — enough to navigate Dûmna's archive, not enough for full independent reading. Identification of Vysandri artifacts and assessment of their operational status. Intelligence on other factions in Kalazym: who they work for, what they have found, what they have not reported up their chains. Safe passage through College network contacts across multiple Inner Nations cities.

**What they demand:** Specific artifacts from Dûmna's archive — targeted items whose significance Tariq will explain only partially. Access to the Forbidden Archive once the keys are located. Information about what each other faction has discovered and reported. Discretion about the College's involvement — specifically, that the Chroniclers not learn what the College is doing here.

**Complications:** The College's information about the Twins is more complete than they have disclosed. Tariq Montara is working from a briefing document that answers questions the company has not yet thought to ask. This is standard College operational procedure, not a betrayal in the sense the company might later frame it. They will frame it that way anyway. The information that was withheld will have mattered.

The briefing document has a second section. It describes the specific scenario the Middle Circle considers the campaign's worst-case outcome: Ordo Draconis interest in Dûmna's archive, confirmed. Tariq has been told to watch for Ordo presence among the other factions. He has not found it. He is watching the wrong factions.

**Faction Clock:**

1. Tariq Montara establishes working contact with the company
2. College researchers arrive in Kalazym under academic cover
3. Secure access to Dûmna's public archive — first reading session
4. Identification and location of the Forbidden Archive entrance
5. Extraction of specific Forbidden Archive materials, with or without the company
6. Research complete — College withdraws with findings, or a cosmological resonance event occurs

## THE ORDO DRACONIS

**[NPC-ONLY FACTION. Player characters cannot be members of the Ordo Draconis. The entry below exists to help the GM run the Ordo as a threat that does not know it is a threat — and does not announce itself as one.]**

The Ordo believes the Second Era ended with an injustice. Not a tragedy, not a necessary cost — an injustice. The greatest Illyan was imprisoned not because it was evil but because five entities decided the outcome of a war before that war had finished. The balance Ilianism describes is the balance those five chose. The Known World operates on that choice. The Ordo has spent three thousand years treating that as a problem requiring correction.

Their interest in the Twins is specific. Dûmna's sealed archive holds Vysandri records of the Second Era's end — primary source material about the Five's work that has been inaccessible since the Third Era began. The Ordo has been assembling incomplete accounts of the Darkness's banishment from fragments across Dead Lands sites for two generations. What Dûmna holds is not another fragment. It is the source record.

Their operative in Kalazym is **Idris Farag**, a research archivist who arrived eleven days ago as part of Borte Khasar's operation — a Chronicler specialist on loan from the King's Hold document collection, requested specifically for his expertise in Vysandri inscription. The assignment is genuine. Borte filed the request six weeks ago. What she does not know is that the specialist who answered it is not the specialist whose name appears on the original Chronicler file. The file was altered before it left King's Hold.

Farag is patient, methodical, and entirely sincere. He has spent fourteen years building a reading of the cosmological evidence that is coherent, internally consistent, and catastrophically wrong in ways that are specific: wrong in the same way the Ordo's most capable members are wrong, which is not through ignorance but through a conclusion that follows from real premises and arrives somewhere the premises cannot support. He is looking for evidence, not a weapon. Whether that distinction matters, given what the Ordo does with evidence, is a question the GM should hold quietly until the moment it is no longer quiet.

He has not made contact with any other faction. He is working.

**What they offer, to parties willing to take it:** Farag is not authorized to recruit, and the Ordo does not reveal itself during Allegro under any normal circumstances. If the party discovers his affiliation and he concludes they cannot be moved against him, he will offer what he has: access to the Chronicler document network he has been using as cover, which contains survey records no other faction in Kalazym has seen. He will frame this as a research partnership. He will not mention the Ordo. The party will have to decide how much they trust a man who has already demonstrated he is not what his credentials say.

**What working with the Ordo costs:** Association with the Ordo, confirmed or suspected, ends the College of Whispers' engagement immediately and permanently. It does the same with the Order of Magnus, if the Order has any presence in the region. The Chroniclers will not protect a cell member if protecting one becomes expensive — the Ordo understands this and has never expected institutional cover from the organizations it infiltrates. Parties that assist Farag in accessing the sealed archive have helped deliver the Known World's most complete primary account of the Darkness's banishment to an organization whose explicit goal is undoing that banishment.

**What exposing the Ordo produces:** If the party discovers Farag's affiliation and reports it to the College, the College's posture shifts in the next interaction and does not shift back. More information. More access. Greater urgency on every subsequent request. Tariq Montara will not explain why. The reason is that confirming Ordo interest in Dûmna's archive is the specific scenario his briefing document treated as the campaign's worst-case outcome. The party does not need to understand why for the consequences to be real.

If the party discovers Farag's affiliation and says nothing — to anyone — the Ordo has an uncontested window. How large that window is depends on the other faction clocks.

**Faction Clock:**

1. Farag maps which tunnel sections he can access under Borte Khasar's authority
2. First deep survey — Farag confirms the sealed archive is real and locates its approach
3. Courier dispatched to the nearest Ordo cell with findings and a resource request
4. Ordo specialists travel to Kalazym under separate cover — academic, mercenary, or pilgrim, depending on what Farag has reported about the town's current composition
5. Farag identifies who holds or is closest to the archive keys
6. Sealed archive accessed — with the keys, through the party, or by methods the arriving specialists bring that Farag did not have

## HOUSE JAEGER — TWO POSITIONS

_The Bellarian crown has reached opposite conclusions about the Twins. Princess Yelena and Prince Andreas are both in Kalazym, through agents, simultaneously._

**Yelena — Frosthold**

Yelena's analysis: the Twins change Bellaria's negotiating position with the Chroniclers if and only if Bellaria establishes control before the Chroniclers document a claim. The obsidian lies in territory Bellaria has more standing to claim than any other nation. A crown-controlled extraction operation, operating under Bellarian licensing rather than Chronicler licensing, would be the first serious challenge to the monopoly in a generation. It might not succeed. Yelena has calculated that the attempt is worth making regardless.

Her agent in Kalazym, **Erwin Brauer**, is a southern-register merchant's son from Wintergate who arrived three weeks ago with a letter of introduction from a crown intelligence front. He is authorized to offer contracts, collect survey data, and ensure that significant findings reach Frosthold before they reach King's Hold.

_What Yelena offers:_ Legal cover for operations in Bellarian frontier territory. Crown letters of introduction that open doors in Bellarian towns. Reasonable payment in coin with no Chronicler documentation. The long-term offer: Bellarian title and land grant for significant service. This is genuine and has been granted before.

_What Yelena demands:_ Survey data submitted to Frosthold simultaneously with or before any other submission. Help establishing a legally defensible Bellarian claim on the railway before other factions get there first. Discretion until the claim can be publicly defended.

**Andreas — Stormkeep**

Andreas's analysis is different and not wrong on its own terms. The existing mountain trade routes through northern Bellaria generate revenue for the northern clans whose political support keeps him in Stormkeep. A functional railway connecting Vysandárien to the Inner Sea — operated by anyone — bypasses those routes. The clans lose revenue. Andreas loses standing. The answer, from where he sits, is that the railway should not become functional.

His operative in Kalazym, **Arslan Belgüt**, is a northern clan professional with a background in keeping Dead Lands approaches clear and northern territories functional — which means he knows how to make things stop working and how to ensure the stoppage reads as accidental. He arrived four days ago. He has not made contact with any other faction. He is assessing.

_What Andreas offers:_ Payment for sabotage operations framed as security work. Protection contracts in the northern territory that no southern-faction operator can credibly provide. Access to northern clan networks that Erwin Brauer cannot reach.

_What Andreas demands:_ Specific actions against the railway's operational infrastructure — framed as "hazard clearances" or "stability assessments," producing reduced functionality. Discretion. No overlapping contracts with Yelena's operation.

**Complications:** Both agents are in Kalazym simultaneously. They are aware of each other. They have not made contact. The company working for both at once is the most immediately profitable option and the most dangerous one, and the danger is not primarily physical.

**Faction Clocks:**

_Yelena:_

1. Erwin Brauer identifies the discovery's full extent and reports to Frosthold
2. A crown surveying team is authorized
3. Bellarian territorial claim formally filed on the railway's western section
4. Crown expedition launched toward the Twins
5. Confrontation with the Chroniclers over jurisdictional authority
6. Bellarian control of the railway — or political defeat

_Andreas:_

1. Arslan Belgüt maps all active faction positions in Kalazym
2. First sabotage operation — reads as accidental
3. Northern clan pressure applied to the magistrate's office
4. Systematic disruption of all non-Bellarian operations in the tunnel
5. Open conflict with Yelena's operation
6. Railway access sealed — or Arslan removed and the position collapses

## THE MOUNTAIN CLANS OF VYSANDÁRIEN

_Three loosely allied clans who have lived in the Vysandri mountain range for two centuries. They did not build what is in the mountains. They do not know what it is. They know it is theirs._

The clans settled into the range over two centuries — families from the northern margins of various Inner Nations who found that the Vysandri ruins provided structural shelter unavailable elsewhere and that the giants who wandered through could be managed if approached with consistent care. That care has become culture: the giants are given right of way, never startled, never harmed. The clans call them the Old Ones. This is either a coincidence or a preserved memory too fragmented to be useful. It is not both.

Their elder, **Krag Deepcutter**, comes to Kalazym twice a year to trade and assess what is moving in and out of town. He arrived a week ago and has not left. He knows something has changed in the western approach tunnels. He does not yet know what. He intends to find out.

**What they offer:** Safe passage through Vysandárien mountain territory — without this, overland travel outside the railway tunnels in this range is genuinely dangerous, not from creatures but from terrain and weather the clans navigate by accumulated knowledge. Guides for specific routes. Warning about which sections of the ruins have recent giant activity.

**What they demand:** Respect for the Old Ones — no violence against giants, no actions that would drive them toward clan territory. Tribute in equipment and coin for use of the mountain approaches. No desecration of specific sites within the ruins that the clans consider sacred. No sharing of information about clan territory or movement patterns.

**Complications:** The clans do not know what the ruins are. A company that discovers the truth about the giants and tells the clans is doing something that cannot be undone. The clans' response to learning that the Old Ones are the degraded remnants of the ruins' builders is not predictable. It might produce cooperation. It might produce something else. The GM should decide privately what that response would be, because the players may make this choice without asking first.

**Faction Clock:**

1. Krag Deepcutter identifies the scale of the new activity and reports to the other clan elders
2. Clan scouts begin monitoring all approach routes to the Twins
3. Formal tribute demand to all parties operating in mountain territory
4. Attack on parties that refuse payment or violate clan terms
5. Clan alliance against the most aggressive outside faction
6. Withdrawal deeper into the range — or a negotiated settlement that requires the company's intervention to hold

## THE CHURCH OF TAR'AMAAN

**[NPC-ONLY FACTION. Player characters cannot be members of the Church of Tar'amaan. The entry below exists to help the GM run the Church as an antagonist.]**

The Church is in Kalazym through three soldiers traveling as pilgrims. Their officer, **Moritz Hessler**, has a devotional name he uses in internal communications and a birth name he uses everywhere else. He is a professional, which is more dangerous in the field than a fanatic. His assessment, reported six days ago to the Archbishop responsible for the western territory: a site of this scale, with intact magical infrastructure, under the control of any party that is not the Church represents an unacceptable strategic liability. The Archbishop has requested clarification of "unacceptable." Hessler's clarification is pending the company's actions in the next two sessions.

The Church wants the Twins either destroyed or controlled. Their theology is unambiguous: the knowledge the Vysandri preserved enabled the magical practice that preceded the Second Era's catastrophe, and maintaining that knowledge is maintaining the conditions for that catastrophe's return. The College of Whispers' presence in Kalazym is, to Hessler, confirmation that the site requires immediate action.

**What they offer, to parties willing to take it:** Payment in Tarans — Cadman currency, worth full value in Val Cadma and occupied Norderenn, subject to significant suspicion everywhere else. Formal Church protection in Cadman-controlled territory. Pardons for past "heresies" — a category the Church defines broadly enough to be useful for anyone with a complicated history. These offers are real. Parties that accept them become assets rather than contractors, and the distinction becomes apparent gradually.

**What they demand:** Access through the railway at specific times for "pilgrim" traffic. Elimination of College of Whispers operatives — framed as protection against dangerous actors. Silence about Church movements. Eventually: cooperation with conversion efforts among the mountain populations.

**What working for the Church costs:** Association marks the company as Tar'amaan-adjacent to every other faction simultaneously. The College of Whispers cuts contact. The mountain clans refuse to deal with any party they believe has Church backing. Yelena stops cooperating with parties she believes are providing the Church intelligence. The Church does not forgive defection. It does not have a process for it.

**Faction Clock:**

1. Hessler maps all active factions and their current positions
2. "Pilgrim" scouts enter the tunnel on a reconnaissance pass
3. Hessler contacts Val Cadma military command for authorization of a larger operation
4. Assassination of the most effective College of Whispers operative in the region
5. Attempted seizure of a railway tunnel section
6. Open military intervention — or withdrawal if the political cost exceeds what Val Cadma has authorized

# GETTING THE COMPANY TO KALAZYM

Five starting positions. Each assumes the company is already a functioning unit — established enough to have a reputation, not so established that work in a frontier Bellarian town is beneath them. The GM and players should select the hook that fits the characters being built, or use it as the foundation for building those characters.

---

## HOOK 1 — THE PATRON WITHOUT A NAME

A representative approaches the company in whichever town they are currently working from. The offer: 200 gold pieces up front, 200 more on delivery of a complete survey of the western approach tunnel past the Crimson Company's third station. The representative's employer is not disclosed. The representative, **Nafi Solevar**, is professional enough that asking the question does not produce an answer, though it does produce a slight reassessment of the company's judgment.

The money is clean. The contract is specific. The identity question is left open.

The employer's actual affiliation becomes visible over the first two sessions, through the instructions that follow the initial contract. By the time the company understands who they have been working for, they have already made several decisions on the assumption of a different answer.

_This hook enters the campaign without a declared faction allegiance. The first relationship the company establishes in Kalazym becomes their initial foothold. It suits players who prefer to assess factions before committing._

---

## HOOK 2 — THE CRIMSON COMPANY DEBT

One or more company members owe the Crimson Company a favor — the specifics determined with the player, large enough to mean something. Ulrik Fendt's message, when it arrives, is characteristically economical:

_You pushed past our depot. Tell us what you found and we call it even. Bring us a full survey and we're ahead._

The implication is accurate. Someone in the company has already been in the tunnel. They are being asked to complete something one of them started.

The GM and that player should work out before session one what was found and what was not reported. That gap is a resource the player holds, one that every faction in Kalazym will eventually want access to.

_This hook establishes an immediate Crimson Company relationship and a specific information asymmetry inside the party. It works well for groups interested in the internal dynamics of a mercenary company navigating competing loyalties._

---

## HOOK 3 — THE ACADEMIC CONNECTION

A College of Whispers contact — known to be College, or not, at the GM's discretion — approaches a company member with an interest in Vysandri history. A new site has been reported in the Kalazym area. The College is funding an independent research expedition and needs security.

Tariq Montara may or may not be the person who makes this approach. The payment is fair. The explicit contract terms are reasonable. The implicit terms will take longer to surface.

_This hook establishes an immediate College relationship and arrives in Kalazym with more historical context about the site than any other faction has shared with the company. It also means the company arrives already associated with the College, which shapes early faction interactions in ways the players will not fully understand until they are inside them._

## HOOK 4 — THE ACCIDENT

The company is in Kalazym — passing through, completing an unrelated contract — when a minor seismic event collapses a section of the lower quarter's storage district. The collapse reveals a previously unknown entrance to the tunnel network. Nobody with standing to claim what lies beyond it is present. The company is.

The gap between "we found this" and "anyone else finds out we found this" is measured in hours.

_This hook provides maximum initial freedom and no initial faction relationships. Everything gets built in play. It rewards groups who want to approach the sandbox without prior commitments, and it creates early resource-management pressure: the company must secure what they found before the window closes, making every decision in the first session consequential._

---

## HOOK 5 — THE OPEN COMPETITION

Word is already out. Three factions — the Chroniclers, the Longkeel Consortium, and a representative claiming to act for one of the Bellarian princes — have posted contracts in Kalazym simultaneously, all for survey work in the same tunnel system. The postings are public. Every faction knows the others are recruiting.

The company chooses which contract to accept. Or attempts to accept two. Or accepts one and takes meetings with the other two without disclosing the first.

_This hook is the most transparent about the campaign's structure — players understand immediately that their choices have consequences and that other parties are pursuing the same objectives. It works best for groups who prefer informed decision-making from the start and are interested in faction politics as a primary mode of play._
