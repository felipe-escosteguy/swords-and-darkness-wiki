# **CHAPTER 15: PRIEST SPELLS**

Priest magic is not studied, but requested.

Every morning, after adequate rest, a priest kneels in prayer and petitions their deity — or the collective force of their faith — for the spells they will carry that day. Under ordinary circumstances, the petitions are granted. Priests who violate the tenets of their faith discover quickly what "ordinary circumstances" means: minor infractions result in spells being withheld for a period the GM determines; serious offenses or repeated violations draw divine retribution.

A priest's access to spells is defined by spheres of influence — the domains their deity claims. A priest with **major access** to a sphere may pray for spells of any level within it. A priest with **minor access** is restricted to spells of 3rd level or lower. No priest draws freely from every sphere — the list of major and minor access is established by the priest's faith and recorded in Chapter 3\.

**Reversible spells** are listed together in a single entry, but each version counts as a separate memorized spell. A priest who wants both *Cure Light Wounds* and *Cause Light Wounds* must spend two spell slots.

**Holy symbol.** A priest's holy or unholy symbol serves as a material component for many spells and is never consumed in casting unless the spell entry explicitly states otherwise.

The component system established in Chapter 13 applies fully to priest spells. The Verbal, Somatic, and Material tags function identically; the potency boost and double-cast mechanics remain available to priest casters via material components, as normal. Sorcerer characters in a party — who substitute hit points for material components — apply the daily cap by Wisdom score rather than Intelligence; see Chapter 13 for the full table.

## **1ST-LEVEL SPELLS**

**ANIMAL FRIENDSHIP** *(Enchantment)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Animal | **CT:** 1 hour | **Range:** 9 m | **Duration:** Permanent | **Area:** 1 animal | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** Will (negates)

This spell may only be cast on a neutrally aligned, mundane animal with Intelligence 1–4. If the animal fails its Will save, it sits quietly while the casting completes. The caster must genuinely intend to befriend the creature — any ulterior motive registers to the animal during the hour of casting, and the spell fails.

Befriended animals are loyal to the caster and obey simple commands. The caster may teach the animal three specific tricks per point of Intelligence: short, one-word commands — attack, guard, fetch — each requiring a week to learn and all taught within 3 months of the bonding. The caster can maintain bonds with animals totalling up to twice his level in HD. Animals left unattended for more than a week revert to their natural state and the spell ends.

*Material component:* the caster's holy or unholy symbol and a piece of food preferred by the target animal.

**BLESS / CURSE** *(Conjuration)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** All | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** 55 m | **Duration:** 6 rounds | **Area:** 15-m cube | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

*Bless* raises morale, attack rolls, and saving throws against fear effects by \+1 for all allies within the area. Allies already engaged in melee are unaffected. PCs receive the attack and save bonuses; the morale bonus applies only to NPC allies and hirelings.

Optionally, a single item up to 500 g per caster level may be blessed rather than the area. A blessed weapon is treated as enchanted for the purpose of striking evil incorporeal creatures and gains a \+1 attack bonus against evil foes for the duration — but only if the weapon carries no existing magical effect. Missiles may be blessed; bows and crossbows do not transfer the blessing to their ammunition.

The reverse, *Curse*, applies a −1 penalty to morale and attack rolls against opponents not yet engaged in melee. A cursed item is correspondingly more effective against good-aligned creatures.

*Material component:* a vial of holy water (for *Bless*) or unholy water (for *Curse*), sprinkled during casting.

**COMBINE** *(Evocation)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** All | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** Concentration | **Area:** Circle of 3–5 priests | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** None

All participating priests must have this spell memorized. The highest-level priest stands at the centre of a circle of two to four others, and all cast *Combine* simultaneously. The central priest then affects undead and casts secondary spells from memory as if they were one level higher per priest in the circle — between 2 and 4 levels of enhancement.

The circle priests must concentrate each round, losing shield and Dexterity bonuses to AC. The spell ends if any circle priest is damaged or their concentration breaks. Damaging the central priest does not end it. If *Combine* ends while a secondary spell is being cast, that spell is lost as if the caster had been struck. Secondary spells already completed retain their enhanced effects even if *Combine* later ends.

The GM may require circle priests to share a faith, a pantheon, or an alignment. For allied priests of closely shared cause, shared faith is usually sufficient.

**COMMAND** *(Enchantment)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Charm | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 27 m | **Duration:** 1 round | **Area:** 1 creature | **Comp:** V | **Save:** Will (special)

The caster issues a single-word command to one creature, stated clearly as a single action the creature can understand. The creature obeys for 1 round. Typical commands: *back, halt, flee, run, stop, fall, go, leave, surrender, sleep, rest*. Commanding a creature to *die* sends it into a catatonic state for 1 round.

Creatures with Intelligence 13 or higher, or 6 or more HD, are allowed a Will save. *Command* has no effect on the undead.

**CREATE WATER / DESTROY WATER** *(Transmutation)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Elemental (Water) | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** 27 m | **Duration:** Permanent | **Area:** Up to 15 L/level, 750 L max | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

*Create Water* produces fresh, pure water within the area. Empty containers are required; without them the water flows to the lowest available point. The water is magical for its first round — dispel magic can unmake it during that window. Afterward it becomes ordinary water.

The reverse, *Destroy Water*, eliminates a like quantity of water within the same range, leaving no trace.

Water cannot be created or destroyed inside a living or undead creature of any kind. Water weighs roughly 1 kg per litre.

*Material component:* a drop of water to create; a pinch of dust to destroy.

**CURE LIGHT WOUNDS / CAUSE LIGHT WOUNDS** *(Necromancy)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Healing | **CT:** 5 | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** Permanent | **Area:** 1 creature | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** None

*Cure Light Wounds* restores 1d8 HP to the creature touched. The spell cannot affect creatures without a corporeal body, the undead, non-living creatures, or extra-planar entities. Only existing wounds are healed; the spell offers no protection against future damage.

The reverse, *Cause Light Wounds*, requires a successful touch attack and inflicts 1d8 HP of damage. Treat wounds so caused as ordinary injuries.

**DETECT EVIL / DETECT GOOD** *(Lesser Divination)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** All | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** 1 turn \+ 5 rounds/level | **Area:** 3 m × 110 m path | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

Within the area, the caster detects evil auras radiating from creatures, objects, or the environment. The caster must stop and concentrate for at least one round on a specific target to detect an aura and gauge its strength — dim, faint, moderate, strong, or overwhelming. Exact alignment is never revealed. Most Prime Material creatures don't radiate an aura unless they are fully devoted to an alignment, are 9 HD or higher with appropriate intent, or are intelligent undead or extra-planar creatures.

The priest's version additionally allows a vague one-word impression of the source's intent — eager, hateful, cruel, kind, and so on — when an aura is detected. Against overwhelming auras, the priest has a 10% chance per caster level to detect whether the source leans toward law, chaos, or neutrality.

The reverse, *Detect Good*, functions identically but reveals good auras.

*Material component:* the caster's holy or unholy symbol, held out before casting.

**DETECT MAGIC** *(Universal)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** All | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** 1 turn | **Area:** 3 m × 27 m path | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

The caster detects magical auras within the area, scanning a 60-degree arc per round. Aura intensity — dim, faint, moderate, strong, or overwhelming — is discernible, and the caster has a 10% chance per caster level to identify the sphere of a detected effect (not its school). At least 30 cm of stone, 2.5 cm of metal, or 1 m of wood or earth blocks the spell. Multiple schools in the same area or overwhelmingly powerful auras may produce confusing, incomplete, or conflicting results.

*Material component:* the caster's holy or unholy symbol.

**DETECT POISON** *(Lesser Divination)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Divination | **CT:** 4 | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** 1 turn \+ 1 round/level | **Area:** Special | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

The caster checks for poison in one object or up to a 1.5 m cube of material per round. There is a 5% chance per caster level to identify the exact type.

*Material component:* a strip of specially blessed vellum, which turns black if poison is present.

**DETECT SNARES & PITS** *(Lesser Divination)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Divination | **CT:** 4 | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** 4 rounds/level | **Area:** 3 m × 12 m path | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

The caster detects natural or primitively constructed traps in the path ahead: snares, pits, deadfalls, quicksand, sinkholes, unsafe rock faces, and similar hazards made of natural materials by animals, plants, or people. Mechanical dungeon traps and magical wards are beyond the scope of this spell.

*Material component:* the caster's holy or unholy symbol.

**ENDURE HEAT / ENDURE COLD** *(Transmutation)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Protection | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** 1.5 hours/level | **Area:** 1 creature | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** None

One chosen version is selected at casting: *Endure Cold* allows the recipient to withstand temperatures as low as −35°C without discomfort. *Endure Heat* extends tolerance up to 55°C. More extreme temperatures still inflict 1 point of damage per hour of exposure. If the recipient takes magical cold or heat damage, the first 10 points are absorbed — but the spell ends regardless of which version is in effect when hit. The two versions cancel each other.

**ENTANGLE** *(Transmutation)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Plant | **CT:** 4 | **Range:** 72 m | **Duration:** 1 turn | **Area:** 12-m cube | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** Reflex (half)

Plants within the area wrap, grasp, and twist to entangle creatures. Any creature entering or moving through the area must make a Reflex save or be held fast, unable to act, for the duration. A successful save allows movement at 3 m per round or one action per round; any creature remaining in the area must save again each round.

The spell requires some vegetation to function and cannot be cast in a completely barren area. Existing plants stretch to their limits — roots may emerge from ceilings or walls in underground settings where the GM judges plant roots are nearby. Sparse vegetation may grant up to a \+4 bonus to saves.

*Material component:* the caster's holy or unholy symbol.

**FAERIE FIRE** *(Transmutation)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Weather | **CT:** 4 | **Range:** 72 m | **Duration:** 4 rounds/level | **Area:** 1 m² per level within 12-m radius | **Comp:** V, M | **Save:** None

Creatures and objects within the area glow with faint light — blue, green, or violet, the caster's choice — as bright as a small candle. The caster may affect one man-sized or two small-sized creatures per caster level. Glowing targets are visible from 72 m in darkness and 36 m otherwise. Attackers gain a \+2 bonus to hit in darkness or moonlight, \+1 in normal conditions. Invisible creatures can be affected; incorporeal, ethereal, and gaseous creatures cannot. Multiple castings of this spell extend the area but do not increase the brightness.

*Material component:* a small chunk of foxfire.

**INVISIBILITY TO ANIMALS** *(Transmutation)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Animal | **CT:** 4 | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** 1 turn \+ 1 round/level | **Area:** 1 creature/level | **Comp:** S, M | **Save:** None

Each creature touched during casting — up to one per caster level — goes undetected by non-magical animals with Intelligence below 6\. The effect suppresses sound and scent as well as sight with respect to animals. The spell ends for a given subject if they speak or make a hostile action against an animal; it remains in effect for others.

*Material component:* holly rubbed over each subject.

**INVISIBILITY TO UNDEAD** *(Abjuration)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Necromantic | **CT:** 4 | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** 6 rounds | **Area:** 1 creature | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** Will (special)

The recipient goes undetected by undead. Undead with more than 4 HD are allowed a Will save. A priest who is the subject of this spell cannot turn undead that cannot perceive them. The spell ends immediately if the recipient performs any hostile action — attacking, or forcing a saving throw on an affected creature. Casting non-hostile spells such as *Cure Light Wounds* or *Chant* does not break the effect.

*Material component:* the caster's holy or unholy symbol.

**LIGHT / DARKNESS** *(Transmutation)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Sun | **CT:** 4 | **Range:** 110 m | **Duration:** 1 hour \+ 1 turn/level | **Area:** 6-m radius globe | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** Reflex (special)

*Light* creates torch-strength illumination centred on a point within range or attached to a creature or object. If cast on an unwilling creature, a failed Reflex save means the light appears in the air directly behind them. If targeted at a creature's eyes, a failed Reflex save renders it blind for the duration. Objects beyond the glow appear as shadowy forms. Light cancels magical darkness of equal strength on contact, and vice versa. The caster may end the spell with a command word.

The priest's *Darkness* reverse plunges the area into complete darkness for half the normal duration. Only a light source at least as bright as full daylight remains visible inside it.

**LOCATE ANIMALS OR PLANTS** *(Lesser Divination)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Divination (Animal, Plant) | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** 90 m \+ 18 m/level | **Duration:** 1 round/level | **Area:** 6 m × 18 m/level path | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

The caster concentrates on one specific type of animal or plant and learns whether any exist within range — along with direction, distance, and approximate numbers. The caster can only detect subjects directly ahead in a straight path but may shift direction once per round during the duration. Common local specimens are 50% likely to be present; uncommon 30%; rare 15%; very rare 5%.

*Material component:* the caster's holy or unholy symbol.

**MAGICAL STONE** *(Enchantment)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Combat | **CT:** 4 | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** 30 minutes or until used | **Area:** 3 pebbles | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

The caster enchants up to three small pebbles. They count as \+1 weapons for striking creatures requiring magical weapons to hit, and deal 1d4 damage (2d4 against undead). All three can be thrown in one round, with a range of 27 m ignoring range penalties.

*Material components:* the caster's holy or unholy symbol and 1–3 ordinary pebbles.

**PASS WITHOUT TRACE** *(Enchantment)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Plant | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** 1 turn/level | **Area:** 1 creature | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

The recipient moves through any natural terrain — mud, snow, dust — without leaving footprints or scent. Normal tracking becomes impossible, though the ground where the creature has passed radiates a detectable magical aura for 1d6 turns afterward.

*Material component:* a sprig of pine or evergreen, burned to ash and scattered during casting.

**PROTECTION FROM EVIL / PROTECTION FROM GOOD** *(Abjuration)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Protection | **CT:** 4 | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** 3 rounds/level | **Area:** 1 creature | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

This spell creates an invisible magical shield with three simultaneous effects:

**Hinder Evil Attacks.** Attack rolls from evil creatures — or those enchanted by evil forces — suffer a −2 penalty, and saves against their attacks gain a \+2 bonus.

**Block Extra-Planar Contact.** Conjured animals, summoned monsters, and extra-planar creatures cannot make physical contact with the protected creature. Natural attacks auto-fail and the creature recoils. Magic resistance may allow a creature to overcome this. If the protected creature makes a melee attack against such a creature or forces the shield against them, this effect ends.

**Block Mind Control.** Direct mental control and possession attempts are blocked. Charm effects are not blocked, but domination, coercion, and body possession are. An already-possessing life force cannot be expelled.

The reverse, *Protection From Good*, applies the hinder effect against good creatures; effects two and three remain unchanged.

*Material components:* for *Protection From Evil*, a 1 m circle of powdered silver and burning incense drawn on the ground during casting. For the reverse, a circle of powdered iron and smoldering dung.

**PURIFY FOOD & DRINK / PUTREFY FOOD & DRINK** *(Transmutation)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** All | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** 27 m | **Duration:** Permanent | **Area:** 1 cubic metre/level | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** None

*Purify Food & Drink* restores spoiled, rotted, contaminated, or otherwise non-edible food and drink — meat, bread, fruit, vegetables, water, beer, wine — to an edible state. It also destroys unholy water. It cannot prevent future decay, and it has no effect on creatures, potions, or other substances.

The reverse, *Putrefy Food & Drink*, spoils good food and drink and destroys holy water within the area. It similarly has no effect on creatures, potions, or other substances.

**REMOVE FEAR / CAUSE FEAR** *(Abjuration)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Charm | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 9 m | **Duration:** Special | **Area:** 1 creature per 4 levels | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** Will (special)

*Remove Fear* grants affected creatures a \+4 bonus to saves against fear attacks for 1 turn. If a creature is already under a magical fear effect, it immediately receives a new Will save with the \+4 bonus. Casting the reverse automatically counters *Remove Fear* on a target.

The reverse, *Cause Fear*, forces one creature within range to flee at maximum speed for 1d4 rounds. The creature is entitled to a Will save to resist. Casting *Remove Fear* on an affected creature automatically ends the effect. Neither version affects the undead directly, but either can counter a fear effect caused by an undead creature.

**SANCTUARY** *(Abjuration)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Protection | **CT:** 4 | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** 2 rounds \+ 1 round/level | **Area:** 1 creature | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

An invisible safe haven surrounds the protected creature. Any enemy attempting to physically attack or directly target the protected creature with a harmful effect must make a Will save. Failure means the enemy abandons their current action and, for the duration, completely ignores the protected creature — as if they have forgotten their existence. Enemies who never attempt to attack are unaffected. Area spells such as *Fireball* or *Ice Storm* that cover an area rather than targeting a specific creature bypass this protection.

The spell ends immediately if the protected creature attempts to harm an enemy. Non-harmful actions — casting divinations, using *Chant*, curing allies — do not break the protection.

*Material components:* a small silver mirror and the caster's holy or unholy symbol.

**SHILLELAGH** *(Transmutation)* **Level 1** | **Sphere:** Combat, Plant | **CT:** 2 | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** 4 rounds \+ 1 round/level | **Area:** 1 oak club or staff | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

The caster's wooden club or staff becomes fully magical for the duration. While in the caster's hands, it gains a \+1 bonus to attack rolls and deals 2d4 damage against man-sized and smaller creatures, or 1d4 \+ 1 against large creatures. The weapon is unharmed by the enchantment.

*Material components:* a shamrock leaf and the caster's holy or unholy symbol.

## **2ND-LEVEL SPELLS**

**AID** *(Necromancy, Conjuration)* **Level 2** | **Sphere:** Necromantic | **CT:** 5 | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** 1 round \+ 1 round/level | **Area:** 1 creature | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

The caster infuses one creature — or themselves — with divine energy: a \+1 bonus to attack rolls and saving throws, plus 1d8 temporary HP for the duration. These temporary HP may push the total above the creature's normal maximum; they are lost first in combat and cannot be cured or healed away.

*Material components:* a tiny strip of white cloth with adhesive ends and the priest's holy or unholy symbol.

**AUGURY** *(Lesser Divination)* **Level 2** | **Sphere:** Divination | **CT:** 2 rounds | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** Special | **Area:** Special | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

The caster petitions for divine insight, asking whether a specific action taken in the next half hour will benefit or harm the party. Base chance of receiving a useful reply: 70% \+ 1% per caster level. Replies are brief — yes, no, I don't know, or a short cryptic riddle of no more than five words.

The GM is advised to limit this spell to once per adventure, or once per week at most. Deities do not enjoy being interrupted constantly.

*Material component:* a set of fortune-telling tokens worth at least 1,000 gp, not consumed in casting.

**BARK SKIN** *(Transmutation)* **Level 2** | **Sphere:** Protection, Plant | **CT:** 5 | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** 4 rounds \+ 1 round/level | **Area:** 1 creature | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

The recipient's skin becomes as tough as bark, providing a base AC of 14 with an additional \+1 per 4 caster levels, and a \+1 bonus to all saving throws except magic. This spell does not function over armour or magical protection of any kind, but stacks with Dexterity bonuses and shields.

*Material components:* the caster's holy or unholy symbol and a handful of bark from an old oak tree.

**CHANT** *(Conjuration)* **Level 2** | **Sphere:** All | **CT:** 2 rounds | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** Duration of chanting | **Area:** 9-m radius | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** None

While the caster chants without pause, all allies within the area receive a \+1 bonus to attack rolls, damage, and saving throws, and all opponents within the area suffer a −1 penalty to the same. If the caster is damaged or concentration is interrupted, the spell ends. Multiple *Chant* effects do not stack, but a second priest of the same faith casting *Prayer* during an active *Chant* increases the combined effect to \+2 and −2 for as long as both spells remain in force.

**CHARM** *(Enchantment)* **Level 2** | **Sphere:** Animal | **CT:** 5 | **Range:** 72 m | **Duration:** Special | **Area:** 1 person or mammal | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** Will (negates)

The target regards the caster as a trusted friend and ally unless it succeeds at a Will save. (If the target took damage from the caster or their allies in the same round the spell was cast, it gains a \+1 bonus to its save per point of damage received.) A charmed creature obeys verbal instructions within reason — it will not take suicidal actions or act wholly against its nature — and retains full memory of everything it does while charmed.

The creature is allowed a new Will save at regular intervals determined by its Intelligence:

| Intelligence | Time Between Saves |
| ----- | ----- |
| 3 or less | 3 months |
| 4–6 | 2 months |
| 7–9 | 1 month |
| 10–12 | 3 weeks |
| 13–14 | 2 weeks |
| 15–16 | 1 week |
| 17 | 3 days |
| 18 | 2 days |
| 19+ | 1 day |

The spell ends if the caster or their allies openly harm the subject, or a new save succeeds. The GM determines the exact moment each check occurs.

The priest's version also targets naturally occurring, non-magical mammals with Intelligence 1–4. An animal charmed in this way may be kept on long-term if cast alongside *Animal Friendship*.

**DETECT CHARM / UNDETECTABLE CHARM** *(Lesser Divination)* **Level 2** | **Sphere:** Divination | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** 1 turn | **Area:** 1 creature/round | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** Will (special)

*Detect Charm* reveals whether a creature is under a charm, compulsion, hypnosis, or similar effect. The subject is allowed a Will save; success means the caster learns nothing. On a failed save, the caster knows the creature is under influence and has a 5% chance per caster level to identify the exact nature of the effect. When multiple charms affect a creature simultaneously, the exact natures cannot be distinguished.

The reverse, *Undetectable Charm*, masks charm detection on a single creature for 24 hours or until *Dispel Magic* succeeds.

**DUST DEVIL** *(Conjuration)* **Level 2** | **Sphere:** Elemental (Air) | **CT:** 2 rounds | **Range:** 27 m | **Duration:** 2 rounds/level | **Area:** 1.5-m × 1.2-m cone | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** None

The caster summons a weak air elemental — a dust devil — that travels on verbal command but disperses if it moves more than 27 m from the caster. The caster is not immune to its effects.

The dust devil has AC 16, MV 18, and 2 HD, and attacks once per round with Attack Bonus \+1, dealing 1d4 damage. In dusty or debris-strewn areas, it kicks up a cloud with a 3-m diameter centred on itself. Creatures caught in the cloud are blinded for 1 round after leaving it. Spellcasters inside the cloud must succeed at a Fortitude save to maintain concentration while casting.

**ENTHRALL** *(Enchantment)* **Level 2** | **Sphere:** Charm | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** Special | **Area:** 27-m radius | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** Will (negates)

The caster must speak uninterrupted for the full casting time and continue speaking for the enchantment to hold — up to one hour. Creatures in the area who understand the language and have 3 or fewer HD or Wisdom below 16 must make a Will save. Failures are peacefully enthralled, standing motionless and ignoring everything except the caster's words. About half of those who succeed their save also become hostilely enthralled — compelled to listen but heckling and booing throughout. The rest are unaffected and act normally.

Creatures from a hostile faction or opposing religion receive a \+4 bonus to their save. If the crowd is attacked, the spell ends and all enthralled listeners react against the attackers with a −10 penalty to the initial reaction roll.

A concerted effort to rouse a specific enthralled individual grants it a new save every third round. Enthralled creatures who succeed this second save join the hecklers half the time.

**FIND TRAPS** *(Lesser Divination)* **Level 2** | **Sphere:** Divination | **CT:** 5 | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** 3 turns | **Area:** 3 m × 27 m path | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** None

The caster detects any active trap — mundane or magical — directly ahead, regardless of how it has been concealed. A trap, for this spell, is any device or magical effect intentionally designed to inflict a sudden, unexpected, and harmful result as a direct consequence of the victim's action. Alarm spells, *Glyphs of Warding*, and mechanical spring traps all qualify. Natural hazards and ambushes do not.

The caster immediately learns whether a detected trap is magical, mechanical, or both, but not its effect or how to disarm it. Careful examination — spending a round studying the trap — reveals what actions or inactions trigger it.

**HEAT METAL / CHILL METAL** *(Transmutation)* **Level 2** | **Sphere:** Elemental (Fire) | **CT:** 5 | **Range:** 36 m | **Duration:** 7 rounds | **Area:** Special | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** Special

Elven mail is unaffected. Magical iron or steel items receive a Fortitude save versus magical fire to avoid the effect. *Protection From Fire* or equivalent resistance negates the spell's effects.

Creatures in contact with the heated metal take damage based on the escalating rounds:

| Round | Metal State | Damage |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| 1 | Warm | None |
| 2 | Hot | 1d4 |
| 3–5 | Searing | 2d4 |
| 6 | Hot | 1d4 |
| 7 | Warm | None |

| 1d10 | Location | Disability |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| 1–4 | Hand/Foot | Unusable for 2d4 days |
| 5–9 | Body | Disabled for 1d4 days |
| 10 | Head | Unconscious for 1d4 turns |

The reverse, *Chill Metal*, freezes ferrous metal through the same seven-round progression. A resist cold spell or cold resistance negates it. Exposure to fire damage counters it on a point-for-point basis.

*Material component:* the caster's holy or unholy symbol.

## **3RD-LEVEL SPELLS**

**ANIMATE DEAD** *(Necromancy)* **Level 3** | **Sphere:** Necromantic | **CT:** 5 rounds | **Range:** 9 m | **Duration:** Permanent | **Area:** Special | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

The caster animates skeletons or zombies from the remains of dead humans, humanoids, human-adjacent ancestries, giants, or — optionally — animals. Animated undead obey simple commands but cannot be dismissed by *Dispel Magic*. The total HD of undead created cannot exceed the caster's level.

Human-scale creatures with only 1 HD in life become 1 HD skeletons or 2 HD zombies regardless of prior class. Creatures with 1 HD or more retain their HD as skeletons and gain 1 HD as zombies. Undead created this way have none of the special abilities they possessed in life.

Evil casters may use this spell freely. Neutral casters may animate the bodies of fallen enemies from non-player ancestries or do so with express permission from the deceased (via *Speak With Dead*). Good casters prefer to animate animals and approach humanoid bodies only with the same courtesy. Animating a corpse being prepared for *Raise Dead* is universally condemned — the soul requires its original body to return; if that body is animated, resurrection requires *Reincarnate* or *Wish*.

The practical reality: undead smell. Animals avoid them unless specifically trained. Hirelings routinely refuse work with parties known to travel with undead. Most civilizations regulate undead creation; some mandate immediate destruction; even tolerant jurisdictions require registration. Good and lawful casters must either obtain permits where possible or gather bodies only with legal sanction.

*Material component:* a pinch of bone powder or a bone shard.

Three flags before continuing:

**1\. Sphere fidelity and the campaign setting.** FG\&G's sphere list is mechanical but faith-agnostic. Nothing written above conflicts with that — but the sphere tags here are placeholders until Book II locks the theology.

**2\. Animal Growth at level 5\.** FG\&G's spell entry incorrectly lists the level as Priest/1, but the spell table clearly places it at 5th level for priests. The entry is written for the 5th-level position, consistent with 2e precedent.

**3\. I've interpreted this as outdoors range constrained to storm cloud coverage, which preserves the intent without producing an absurdly large mechanical area that strains comprehension at the table. **TRANSMUTE METAL TO WOOD** *(Transmutation)* **Level 7** | **Sphere:** Elemental (Earth) | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** 72 m | **Duration:** Permanent | **Area:** 1 metal object | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** Special

The caster converts a metal object of up to 4.5 kg per caster level into wood. Objects with magical properties have 90% resistance to the effect. Objects carried, held, or worn by a creature receive a Fortitude save equal to the creature's save versus spells. Artefacts and relics cannot be affected. Restoration requires a *Limited Wish*, *Wish*, or equivalent.

Wooden weapons converted from metal suffer −2 to attack and damage rolls and splinter on a natural roll of 1 or 2\. Wooden armour converted from metal loses 2 AC and takes an additional −1 AC penalty each time it is struck with a natural roll of 19 or 20\.

*Material component:* the caster's holy or unholy symbol.

**WIND WALK** *(Transmutation)* **Level 7** | **Sphere:** Elemental (Air) | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** 1 hour/level | **Area:** The caster \+ 1 person per 8 levels | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

The caster and up to three other touched creatures alter their bodies into cloudlike vapour, moving through the air at speeds ranging from movement 6 (manoeuvre class 1\) to 100 km/h at high altitude. Wind walkers appear misty and translucent. They can resume physical form and later return to vapour form at will; each transition takes 1 round and counts against the duration. The caster can end the effect for the whole group or for individuals. During the last round, creatures still in vapour form automatically descend to ground level.

In vapour form, creatures are immune to mundane weapon attacks. No spellcasting is possible while vaporous.

*Material components:* fire and holy or unholy water.
