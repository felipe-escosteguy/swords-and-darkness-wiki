# **CHAPTER 14: WIZARD SPELLS**

Wizard spells combine verbal formulae, precise gestures, and material components to channel arcane energy into reproducible effects. The theory underlying this process is the subject of the Order of Magnus's ongoing research program; the practice predates the Order by centuries.

## **Using This Chapter**

Each spell entry follows a consistent format:

**SPELL NAME** *(School; Reversible if applicable)* **Level** | **Casting Time** | **Range** | **Duration** | **Area** | **Components** | **Save**

Spell description.

**Casting Time** less than 1 round is a penalty to the caster's initiative that round. Casting times of 1 round or more resolve at the end of the stated period.

**Range** of 0 means the spell centers on or affects only the caster. Touch requires physical contact. All other ranges require line of sight to a visible target.

**Components:** V \= verbal (must speak clearly; casting silently or in a whisper halves range, or halves duration for touch and self spells). S \= somatic (both hands free; casting with restricted hands halves area of effect, or gives the target \+4 to their saving throw for single-target spells). M \= material (specified in description; casting without it halves potency or halves duration for utility spells — consumed unless otherwise noted). Components marked **\[Mandatory\]** cannot be omitted or substituted.

Specialist Mages may ignore material component requirements for spells from their school without penalty. All other arcane casters require all listed components for full effect.

**Save:** Will resists mind-affecting effects. Reflex resists area effects and anything avoidable by quick movement. Fortitude resists poison, death, physical transformation, and direct physical trauma. Negate means a successful save cancels all effects. Half means a successful save reduces damage by half. None means no save is permitted.

**Reversible spells** are noted in the heading. The reversed version requires a separate memorized slot. Reversed versions are described at the end of the spell entry.

Spells that grant similar bonuses do not stack — the stronger effect takes precedence. A spell caster can dismiss their own spell by concentrating for a round within casting range.

## **Illusions: Additional Rules**

Illusions manipulate perception — color, light, sound, scent, and temperature. They have no physical substance and cannot support weight, carry objects, or interact physically with the environment. An illusory crate can conceal what is inside it; it cannot be moved without the concealed contents becoming visible.

Illusory creatures fight using the caster's attack statistics, not those of the creature depicted. They take damage as the caster directs and react convincingly to attacks, but possess none of the actual creature's abilities or attacks unless the caster knows and believes those abilities exist.

Disbelieving an illusion is a deliberate act requiring one full round of concentration. A character who successfully disbelieves is immune to its effects and may warn companions, granting them \+4 to their next disbelief attempt. GMs should not force disbelief saving throws unless the illusion is obviously implausible or an ally has warned the character.

Illusory damage is not real, but victims who believe they are injured may die from shock. Track illusory damage separately. A creature reduced to 0 HP by an illusion falls unconscious and must succeed on a System Shock check (Table 1.2). On a success, they regain consciousness in 1d3 turns with all illusory damage removed. On a failure, they die.

## **1st-Level Wizard Spells**

---

**AFFECT NORMAL FIRES** *(Transmutation)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 15 ft/level | **Duration:** 2 rounds/level | **Area:** 10-ft radius | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** None

Choose one effect at casting: alter illumination or extinguish flames.

*Alter Illumination.* Adjust any non-magical fire in the area from ember-dim to double its normal radius. Does not affect fuel consumption or damage. The caster can freely vary the adjustment during the duration. Does not affect magical fires or fire-based creatures.

*Extinguish Flames.* Snuff any non-magical fire in the effective area. The spell ends immediately after this use.

---

**ALARM** *(Abjuration, Evocation)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** 30 ft | **Duration:** 4 hours \+ 30 min/level | **Area:** Up to 20-ft cube | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

Wards an area against entry. Any creature larger than six cubic inches or weighing more than 3 pounds that enters, touches, or contacts the warded area without the password triggers an audible ringing — clearly heard to 60 feet, minus 10 feet per closed door and 20 feet per wall in between. The alarm sounds for one round. Ethereal and astral creatures do not trigger it; invisible, incorporeal, levitating, flying, and gaseous creatures do.

The caster may speak a single word to dismiss the alarm at any time.

*Material component:* A tiny bell and a length of fine silver wire.

---

**ARMOR** *(Conjuration)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** Until dispelled | **Area:** 1 creature | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

Creates an invisible field of magical force that raises the recipient's AC to 14\. This replaces worn armor if the worn armor would provide a lower AC, but stacks with Dexterity and magical bonuses. The spell absorbs up to 8 \+ the caster's level in hit points of damage before dissipating; after that threshold, it ends.

*Material component:* A piece of cured leather.

---

**AUDIBLE GLAMER** *(Illusion)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 60 ft \+ 10 ft/level | **Duration:** 3 rounds/level | **Area:** Hearing range | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** Will negates (disbelief)\*

Creates sounds originating from any point within range — whispering, normal conversation, shouting, or any non-verbal sounds the caster can describe. The volume can range from a whisper to a roar equivalent to 16 or more people. The sound is convincing enough that listeners will react to it as real. Intelligent creatures may attempt to disbelieve.

*Material component:* A bit of wool or a small lump of wax.

---

**BURNING HANDS** *(Transmutation)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** Instantaneous | **Area:** 5-ft arc per 2 levels (max 15 ft) | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** Reflex half

A fan of searing flame jets from the caster's spread fingers. Deals 1 point of fire damage per caster level (maximum 5\) to everything in the arc. Flammable materials in the area ignite. The caster cannot harm themselves with this spell.

---

**CANTRIP** *(Universal)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 10 ft | **Duration:** 1 hour/level | **Area:** Special | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** None

Produces minor magical effects too trivial for formal spell slots — cleaning a surface, lighting a candle, chilling a drink, producing a harmless spark, creating a small sound or smell, slightly altering color, or other effects judged by the GM to deal no damage and produce no lasting change. Each use is a minor action. The effects persist only as long as the caster maintains the spell duration.

---

**CHANGE SELF** *(Illusion)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** 2d6 rounds \+ 2 rounds/level | **Area:** The caster | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** None

Alters the caster's apparent height, weight, facial features, hair, skin tone, and clothing. The caster cannot appear as a specific known individual convincingly enough to fool close associates. Size changes are limited to one foot taller or shorter and roughly 50 pounds of apparent weight difference. The illusion does not alter voice, scent, or touch. Observers who physically handle the caster or scrutinize them closely may attempt a Will save to disbelieve.

---

**CHARM PERSON** *(Enchantment)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 120 ft | **Duration:** Special | **Area:** 1 humanoid | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** Will negates

Causes one humanoid creature (human, non-human, or giant-class creature of roughly human shape) to regard the caster as a trusted friend and ally. The charmed creature will not fight for the caster but will follow reasonable requests, answer questions, and extend trust in ambiguous situations. It will not take actions it considers suicidal or obviously harmful to those it cares about.

The subject is allowed a new save to break the charm at intervals based on Intelligence: INT 3–8, one save per month; INT 9–12, one save per three weeks; INT 13–18, one save per two weeks. Each save is modified by the caster's Charisma reaction modifier (positive for the caster).

The spell applies only to beings with humanoid form and reasoning ability. It does not work on constructs, undead, or creatures without a language.

**CHILL TOUCH** *(Necromancy)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** 3 rounds \+ 1 round/level | **Area:** The caster's hand | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** Fortitude partial

The caster's touch drains life energy. Each successful touch attack (using the caster's Attack Bonus) deals 1d4 damage and drains 1 point of Strength on a failed Fortitude save. Strength loss recovers at 1 point per turn after the spell ends. Against undead, chill touch deals no damage but forces a Will save or causes the undead to flee for 1d4 rounds.

The caster may make one touch attempt per round for the spell's duration.

**COLOR SPRAY** *(Transmutation)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** Instantaneous | **Area:** 5 × 20 ft cone | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** Will negates (special)\*

A burst of clashing colors erupts from the caster's palm. Creatures in the cone are affected based on HD:

* 2 HD or fewer: Unconscious for 2d4 rounds  
* 3–4 HD: Blinded for 1d4 rounds  
* 5+ HD: Stunned for 1 round

A successful Will save negates all effects. The caster can choose to exclude allies from the cone if they are at least 10 feet from the edge.

*Material component:* A pinch each of powder from a red, yellow, and blue gem.

---

**COMPREHEND LANGUAGES** *(Universal; Reversible)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 round | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** 5 rounds/level | **Area:** 1 creature or object | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

The recipient can understand the spoken or written language of any intelligent creature they encounter. The recipient cannot speak or write the language, only comprehend it. The spell works on text — but is blocked by magical encryption (such as Illusory Script).

*Reversed — Confuse Languages:* Makes one target's speech or writing unintelligible for the duration.

*Material component:* A pinch of soot and salt.

---

**DANCING LIGHTS** *(Transmutation)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 40 ft \+ 10 ft/level | **Duration:** 2 rounds/level | **Area:** Special | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

Creates one of the following, chosen at casting: up to four torch-bright lights moving in any pattern within range; up to four 1-foot spheres of dim light moving in formation; or a single glowing humanoid-shaped figure. The caster can move all lights freely within range as a minor action. The lights do not emit heat and do not ignite flammables.

*Material component:* A bit of phosphorus or a glowworm.

---

**DETECT MAGIC** *(Universal)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** 2 rounds/level | **Area:** 10 ft × 60 ft path | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** None

The caster can sense magical emanations ahead of them in a corridor-shaped area. Items, creatures, and locations with active magical properties glow faintly. The caster can identify the school of a detected aura with a successful Intelligence check. Illusions and mind-affecting magic may block detection at the GM's discretion.

---

**DETECT UNDEAD** *(Lesser Divination, Necromancy)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** 3 turns | **Area:** 10 ft × 60 ft path | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

Detects the presence and general direction of undead creatures within the area. Does not reveal exact location, type, or number. Undead hidden behind walls or closed doors can still be detected.

*Material component:* A bit of earth from a grave.

---

**ENLARGE** *(Transmutation; Reversible)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 5 yards/level | **Duration:** 5 rounds/level | **Area:** 1 creature or object | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** Fortitude negates (if unwilling)\*

Increases the size and weight of one creature or non-magical object by up to 10% per caster level (max 200% of original size). Enlarged creatures deal \+1d6 additional damage with melee weapons per doubling of size. Armor must be resized for the effect — magic armor adjusts automatically. The spell cannot enlarge an object already at its maximum possible size.

*Reversed — Reduce:* Shrinks the target by up to 10% per level, to a minimum of 10% of original size. Reduced creatures deal reduced damage accordingly.

*Material component:* A pinch of powdered iron.

---

**ERASE** *(Transmutation)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 30 ft | **Duration:** Permanent | **Area:** 1 scroll or surface | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** None (special)\*

Removes writings — mundane or magical — from up to two pages or the equivalent surface area. Magical writing has a 90% chance of being erased (roll percentile); the chance drops to 30% against Wizard Mark. A successfully erased magical text is permanently destroyed.

---

**FEATHER FALL** *(Transmutation)* **Level 1** | **CT:** ½ (free action)\* | **Range:** 10 ft/level | **Duration:** 1 round/level | **Area:** Special | **Comp:** V | **Save:** None

Can be cast instantly as a free reaction when the caster or an observed creature begins to fall. Affected creatures and objects descend at 2 feet per second regardless of actual fall distance, taking no falling damage when they reach the ground. The spell can affect up to one creature plus one additional creature per two caster levels.

*Note:* The purely verbal component and instant casting time mean this spell can be cast on someone else's initiative, including while surprised.

---

**FIND FAMILIAR** *(Conjuration)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 2d12 hours | **Range:** 1 mile/level | **Duration:** Special | **Area:** Special | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

Summons a small animal to serve as the caster's familiar. The caster cannot specify what animal appears — the GM determines what creature in the area answers the call. Possible familiars include cats, crows, hawks, owls, weasels, lizards, frogs, and similar small animals. Some extremely unusual familiars may have additional properties.

While bonded, the familiar can communicate with the caster at close range and shares their senses. The caster gains a minor benefit specific to the familiar's type (see individual familiar tables in Appendix A). The familiar has the caster's saving throws and uses their hit points, but does not gain additional HP — if killed, the caster takes 2d6 damage and cannot summon another familiar for one year. The familiar departs if the caster dies.

*Material component:* 1,000 gp worth of incense and brazier with charcoal.

**FLOATING DISC** *(Evocation)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 20 ft | **Duration:** 3 turns \+ 1 turn/level | **Area:** Special | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

Creates a slightly concave disk of magical force, 3 feet in diameter, that floats at waist height and follows the caster within 20 feet. It can carry up to 100 pounds per caster level. The disc moves with the caster but cannot ascend or descend stairs or inclines greater than 45 degrees. If the caster moves more than 20 feet from the disc, it disappears and any contents fall to the ground.

*Material component:* A drop of mercury.

**FRIENDS** *(Enchantment)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** 1d4 rounds \+ 1 round/level | **Area:** 60-ft radius | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** Will negates\*

Temporarily improves the caster's apparent Charisma by 2d4 points for reaction rolls and social interactions. Creatures that fail their Will save treat the caster with noticeably greater warmth and openness for the duration. When the spell ends, creatures who were affected and notice the sudden change in their attitude become suspicious or hostile — the degree depends on how they were influenced.

*Material component:* Chalk, lampblack, and vermilion applied to the face.

**GAZE REFLECTION** *(Transmutation)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** 2 rounds/level | **Area:** The caster | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** None

Creates a mirror-like field around the caster that reflects gaze attacks back at their origin. Any creature using a gaze attack against the caster must make a Will save against its own ability. If the save fails, the creature suffers its own gaze attack. The spell does not protect against other attack forms used by gaze-attack creatures.

**GREASE** *(Conjuration)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 10 yards | **Duration:** 3 rounds \+ 1 round/level | **Area:** 10 ft × 10 ft | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** Reflex or Fortitude (special)\*

Coats a surface with viscous magical grease. Creatures moving through or across the area must succeed on a Reflex save or fall prone. Getting up from prone in the greased area requires another Reflex save. The grease is also flammable — it burns for the round it ignites, dealing 1d6 fire damage to anyone in the area before burning off.

*Material component:* A bit of pork fat.

Holds a door, gate, or similar portal shut as if locked and barred. The holder of a wizard lock spell can freely open a held portal. Creatures with at least 4 more HD than the caster can burst through with a successful Strength check. Knock dispels hold portal.

**HYPNOTISM** *(Enchantment)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 | **Range:** 5 yards | **Duration:** 1 round \+ 1 round/level | **Area:** 1d6 creatures | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** Will negates

Captures the attention of 1d6 creatures with 3 HD or fewer by chanting and making rhythmic hand movements. Affected creatures become highly susceptible to suggestion: any request by the caster that seems reasonable (not suicidal or obviously against the target's interests) will be followed for the duration. **IDENTIFY** *(Universal)* **Level 1** | **CT:** 1 hour | **Range:** 0 | **Duration:** 1 round/level | **Area:** 1 item/level | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** None

Reveals the magical properties of items — their function, number of charges (if any), command words, and whether they are cursed. For each item examined, the caster rolls against their chance to Learn Spell (Table 1.4). On success, one property is revealed. Each casting allows multiple rolls during the spell's duration — one per round — until the duration expires or the caster stops.

Cursed items reveal their properties as benign unless the caster succeeds on a separate roll (penalized by the item's curse strength). A false result is possible.

*Material component:* A pearl worth at least 100 gp (consumed) and an owl feather. The caster must not have eaten for 24 hours before casting.

Dramatically enhances the recipient's leaping ability. Running broad jump: 30 feet forward, up to 10 feet high. Standing broad jump: 10 feet. Vertical jump: 10 feet. The recipient lands safely from any height reached by the jump, but not from additional falling afterward.

*Material component:* A grasshopper's hind leg.

Causes a non-magical item to register as magical under detect magic, identify, or similar effects. The caster can specify what school of magic the false aura should appear to belong to. Creatures using identify get a Will save to detect the false aura; on success, they know the aura is fake.

Fires one glowing bolt of pure magical force that strikes unerringly, dealing 1d4+1 force damage. An additional bolt is created for every two levels above 1st: two bolts at 3rd level, three at 5th, four at 7th, five at 9th (maximum five bolts). Multiple bolts can be directed at a single target or distributed among multiple targets within range. Magic missiles never miss and cannot be blocked by mundane obstacles, but are stopped by a successful magic resistance check and by specific protective spells such as shield.

Repairs a single break or tear in a non-magical object up to 1 cubic foot in size per caster level. Two broken pieces must be present. The spell restores the object to its original integrity as if the break never occurred. It does not restore structural damage from prior aging, multiple breaks, or magical damage.

**SYMBOL** *(Conjuration)* **Level 8** | **CT:** Special | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** Special | **Area:** 60-ft radius | **Comp:** V, S, M | **Save:** Special per type\*

**ASTRAL SPELL** *(Universal)* **Level 9** | **CT:** 9 | **Range:** Touch | **Duration:** Special | **Area:** Special | **Comp:** V, S | **Save:** None

Projects the caster's life force into the Astral Plane, leaving their physical body in suspended animation. The astral form is connected to the physical body by a silver cord — if severed by \+5 weapons, certain creatures, or astral winds, the caster dies immediately. If the second body or astral projection is killed, the cord snaps the caster back to their physical body, ending the spell.

Up to seven additional creatures linked in a circle with the caster can project with them. If the caster is killed, all linked travelers become stranded on the Astral Plane.

**WISH** *(Conjuration, Evocation)* **Level 9** | **CT:** Special | **Range:** Unlimited | **Duration:** Special | **Area:** Special | **Comp:** V | **Save:** Special\*

The most powerful spell in mortal spellcasting. A Wish can:

* Duplicate any wizard spell of 8th level or lower, or any priest spell of 6th level or lower, with maximum variable results and no material component requirement under 10,000 gp.  
* Undo magical transformations (polymorph, petrification, cursed scrolls, specific other effects) permanently.  
* Grant a \+1 permanent bonus to one ability score (subject to racial maximums; each bonus beyond the first requires an additional Wish).  
* Create large quantities of valuable material up to 25,000 gp value (the GM determines exact limits based on wording).  
* Grant any effect the GM considers equivalent in power.

**The limits of Wish.** A Wish is powerful, not infinite. The GM is responsible for interpreting it literally — not charitably. A poorly worded Wish produces what was asked for, not what was intended. Wishes that attempt to kill deities, permanently alter fundamental cosmological facts, or undo the results of other Wishes always fail and may have severe consequences.

Casting Wish ages the caster: 3 years if venerable age is 150 years or less, 6 years if 151–250, 9 years if 251–350, and so on. The caster must rest for one full day per three caster levels after casting a Wish before they can act normally.
